How Were the ACE Routes Chosen?

Data-driven selection factors from 64 implemented routes (2019-2025)

Pre-ACE Speed Dominates Selection

Pre-ACE speed is the strongest predictor of route selection with an importance score of 0.5294, showing that routes with lower speeds are prioritized for enforcement. This data-driven approach targets routes where speed enforcement would be most impactful.

Secondary Selection Factors

Express service availability (0.1996) and route length (0.1315) are secondary factors in ACE selection. Geographic location has minimal influence, demonstrating that ACE deployment follows evidence-based criteria rather than political or demographic considerations.

Evidence-Based Deployment Strategy

The analysis demonstrates that ACE deployment follows a systematic, data-driven approach targeting routes where speed enforcement would be most impactful. This validates the MTA's evidence-based policy implementation strategy.

How Do ACE Routes Impact the Speed of Bus Routes?

Before-and-after analysis of ACE effectiveness on bus speeds

ACE Routes Show Consistent Speed Improvements

ACE routes increased from 7.79 to 8.35 mph (+0.56 mph or +7.2%), demonstrating measurable speed improvements across different geographic areas and route types.

Non-ACE Routes Show Slight Decline

Non-ACE routes decreased slightly (-0.27 mph or -2.7%), yielding a raw ACE effect of +0.83 mph when compared directly to ACE routes.

Bias Correction Reveals True Impact

Propensity score matching was used to create comparable control groups, reducing the bias-corrected effect to +0.15 mph, providing a more accurate measure of ACE's true impact.

Varying Effectiveness Across Route Clusters

The analysis reveals varying effectiveness across route clusters, with some groups showing substantial improvements (up to 18.84%) while faster routes showed minimal change.

Statistical Validation of Results

Rigorous statistical testing confirms the significance of ACE effects, validating the evidence-based approach and supporting continued expansion of the program.

CUNY-Area Routes Excel Under ACE

CUNY-area routes performed particularly well under ACE implementation, demonstrating the program's effectiveness in serving educational communities and supporting student transportation needs.

What Predicts Bus Speed Performance?

Random Forest analysis of speed prediction factors

Pre-ACE Speed Dominates All Predictions

Pre-ACE speed overwhelmingly dominates predictions with an importance score of 0.9687, meaning baseline speed is the strongest predictor of future performance regardless of ACE implementation.

Other Factors Have Minimal Influence

All other factors have minimal influence, with Staten Island location (0.0092), route length (0.0082), and various borough indicators showing only marginal predictive power. This indicates that bus speeds are highly persistent over time.
