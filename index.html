How Were the ACE Routes Chosen?

Data-driven selection factors from 64 implemented routes (2019-2025)

Pre-ACE Speed Dominates Selection

Pre-ACE speed is the strongest predictor of route selection with an importance score of 0.5294, showing that routes with lower speeds are prioritized for enforcement. This data-driven approach targets routes where speed enforcement would be most impactful.

Secondary Selection Factors

Express service availability (0.1996) and route length (0.1315) are secondary factors in ACE selection. Geographic location has minimal influence, demonstrating that ACE deployment follows evidence-based criteria rather than political or demographic considerations.

Evidence-Based Deployment Strategy

The analysis demonstrates that ACE deployment follows a systematic, data-driven approach targeting routes where speed enforcement would be most impactful. This validates the MTA's evidence-based policy implementation strategy.

How Do ACE Routes Impact the Speed of Bus Routes?

Before-and-after analysis of ACE effectiveness on bus speeds

ACE Routes Show Consistent Speed Improvements

ACE routes increased from 7.79 to 8.35 mph (+0.56 mph or +7.2%), demonstrating measurable speed improvements across different geographic areas and route types.

Non-ACE Routes Show Slight Decline

Non-ACE routes decreased slightly (-0.27 mph or -2.7%), yielding a raw ACE effect of +0.83 mph when compared directly to ACE routes.

Bias Correction Reveals True Impact

Propensity score matching was used to create comparable control groups, reducing the bias-corrected effect to +0.15 mph, providing a more accurate measure of ACE's true impact.

Varying Effectiveness Across Route Clusters

The analysis reveals varying effectiveness across route clusters, with some groups showing substantial improvements (up to 18.84%) while faster routes showed minimal change.

Statistical Validation of Results

Rigorous statistical testing confirms the significance of ACE effects, validating the evidence-based approach and supporting continued expansion of the program.

CUNY-Area Routes Excel Under ACE

CUNY-area routes performed particularly well under ACE implementation, demonstrating the program's effectiveness in serving educational communities and supporting student transportation needs.

What Predicts Bus Speed Performance?

Random Forest analysis of speed prediction factors

Pre-ACE Speed Dominates All Predictions

Pre-ACE speed overwhelmingly dominates predictions with an importance score of 0.9687, meaning baseline speed is the strongest predictor of future performance regardless of ACE implementation.

Other Factors Have Minimal Influence

All other factors have minimal influence, with Staten Island location (0.0092), route length (0.0082), and various borough indicators showing only marginal predictive power. This indicates that bus speeds are highly persistent over time.

Why CUNY Students Need ACE Expansion

Analysis of ACE benefits for student transportation

Low ACE Coverage on CUNY Routes

Currently, only 16.7% of CUNY routes have ACE, while CUNY campuses are concentrated in slower-speed boroughs like Manhattan (7.1 mph average) compared to Staten Island (13.6 mph).

High-Potential Routes Identified

Using predictive models, the analysis identifies high-potential routes including M9 (BMCC), B11 (Brooklyn College), and Q34 (Queens College), which could see 0.5-0.6 mph speed improvements with ACE implementation.

Sustained Improvements Confirmed

Historical data shows some routes experienced COVID-related slowdowns but have recovered, while 2025 post-ACE data confirms sustained improvements, suggesting ACE expansion could provide consistent transportation benefits for students currently experiencing the city's slowest bus speeds.

What Else is Slowing Down MTA Buses?

Analysis of violations among exempt vehicles

High Rate of Repeat Offenders

The study found 46.9% of exempt vehicles are repeat offenders, with 6,107 chronic offenders (25+ violations), indicating widespread abuse of exempt privileges.

Geographic Hotspots Identified

Violations concentrate in 5 geographic hotspots, primarily in the Bronx and Manhattan, creating systematic bottlenecks that slow down bus operations.

Emergency Vehicles Lead Violations

Emergency vehicles lead in total violations (265,519) followed by commercial vehicles (257,374), suggesting need for better compliance monitoring.

CUNY Routes Heavily Impacted

CUNY routes experienced 121,278 violations across 32,476 unique vehicles, with M101 showing the highest count, directly affecting student transportation.

Targeted Enforcement Strategy

The analysis recommends reviewing exempt status for chronic offenders and targeting enforcement at identified hotspots to reduce systematic violations.

Progressive Penalty Systems

Implementing progressive penalty systems can address widespread abuse of exempt privileges and improve overall bus speed performance.

Systematic Compliance Monitoring

Enhanced monitoring and enforcement of exempt vehicle privileges can significantly improve bus speeds by reducing violations that cause delays.

Comprehensive Solution Approach

Addressing exempt vehicle violations through targeted enforcement, hotspot monitoring, and progressive penalties can create a comprehensive solution to improve MTA bus speeds.

Machine Learning Validity

ML models to predict bus speeds and evaluate ACE effectiveness

Comprehensive Model Training

We trained four different types of models on data from 330 bus routes, using previous speeds, route characteristics, and location information to make predictions about bus performance.

Strong Model Performance

All the models performed quite well, with the linear regression model coming out slightly ahead - it could predict speeds with about 85% accuracy and typical errors of less than 1 mph.

Feature Engineering Success

The models successfully incorporated multiple data sources including route characteristics, geographic information, and historical performance to create reliable predictions.

Rigorous Validation Methods

Using matched control group analysis, we ensured that our models could accurately evaluate whether ACE actually works by controlling for selection bias and other confounding factors.

Automated Route Ranking

The machine learning models enable automated ranking of routes by improvement potential, providing data-driven recommendations for ACE implementation.

Deployment Readiness Validation

Comprehensive deployment readiness checks ensure all systems are ready for ACE implementation, with models validated across different scenarios and route types.

Feature Importance Insights

Feature importance analysis reveals which factors most strongly predict bus speeds, helping identify the most effective strategies for improving transportation performance.

Solution

Targeted ACE implementation and exempt vehicle monitoring

Targeted ACE Implementation

Implement ACE to the M9, B11, and Q34 routes, as they had the greatest prediction of speed improvement. These high-potential routes will provide the most significant benefits for CUNY students.

Exempt Vehicle Monitoring

Review exempt status for chronic violation offenders and implement monitoring to ensure exempt vehicles are complying with regulations. By implementing both solutions, bus routes can be faster and more reliable for CUNY students.
